About

Yakup Işık

I am a psychologist, researcher, and translator working at the intersection of psychoanalytic thought, technology, and social justice. My clinical practice spans a decade of work with marginalized communities — LGBTQ+ individuals, people living with HIV, and Romani women navigating the intersection of poverty and stigma.

My recent research explores whether artificial intelligence can participate in the delicate process of mentalization — the capacity to understand behavior in terms of underlying mental states. As part of this work, I developed Mindful Reflections, an AI-assisted psychological support platform, funded by an OpenAI Researcher Access Grant.

I hold degrees in Psychology (Istanbul University), Forensic Sciences (Istanbul University), and Information Technologies (Sabancı University). I am currently pursuing advanced training in clinical psychology and psychoanalytic psychotherapy in Europe.
